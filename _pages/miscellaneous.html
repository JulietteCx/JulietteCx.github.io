The Ultimate Chocolate Cake Recipe

I love chocolate cakes. I try to make one for each birthday. For a really long time (and I probably mean for the last ten years) I have been failing at making them as I wanted, i.e., not too sweet, crispy at the top and slightly melting at the bottom. After years of experimenting, here's the ultimate recipe:

Ingredients

  • 200g chocolate (I take it with 70% cocoa)
  • 100g butter
  • 60g flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 10g of sugar (literally)

Steps

1. Preheat the oven in fan mode at 180°C.

2. Grease a plate (I have a 20cm-squared glass one).

3. Break the chocolate in tiny pieces.

4. Dice the butter in small cubes.

5. In a double boiler, melt the chocolate.

6. When the chocolate is melted, add small pieces of butter, one after another.

7. In the meantime, whip up the eggs (combined, yolk and white together) until the preparation triples in volume.

8. When all the butter is incorporated, add the flour to the chocolate preparation (this is key!).

9. Combine the two preparations by adding the chocolate mixture to the eggs. Mix very delicately until the preparation is homogenous. Avoid overmixing to prevent the eggs from collapsing.

10. Pour the preparation into the greased plate.

11. Bake for 20 minutes, or until you can slide a knife in and it comes out clean.

You're done!
